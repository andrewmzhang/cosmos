What is a Globular Cluster?

Globular Clusters are spherical collections of stars that orbit in the halo of a galaxy. The objective of our project is to download and plot the data of Messier 80 on the Color Magnitude diagram.

How are stars formed?

Stars begin as vast clouds of cold molecular hydrogen and helium from the Big Bang. In addition to hydrogen and helium, these clouds are filled with heavier elements from stars that died millions of years ago. The force of gravity in the star is eventually shaken, causing the cloud to collapse. At the center of the clumps, the material starts increasing in heat and density. Once the cloud generates enough pressure and temperature, deuterium fusion, a heavier isotope of hydrogen, starts forming. This causes the cloud to collapse, which prepares it to enter the main sequence phase of a star.

 

 Stellar Evolution

 Stellar Evolution is when a star goes through various drastic changes throughout its  lifetime. Their lifetime ranges from a few million years (most massive stars) to  trillions of years (least massive stars), which, surprisingly, is longer than the age of  the universe!  Initially, the gas of cloud (previously mentioned) starts its life as a  main-sequence star. The core becomes composed of copious amounts of helium,  gradually growing in size, until it reaches the red giant phase.Once a star (ex. the  Sun) has used its nuclear fuel, it collapses into a dense white dwarf with the outer  layers scattered as a planetary nebula. Stars ten or more times the mass of the Sun  explode into a supernova as the core collapses into a dense neutron star or black      hole.

 Color Magnitude Diagram and H-R diagram

The Hertzsprung-Russell diagram, or H-R diagram, is a scatter graph of stars showing the relationship between the absolute magnitude, and the temperature of a star. The color magnitude diagram is a subset of the H-R diagram, but relates the color of the star to temperature and age. A blue star would be considered hotter and younger, and a red star would be considered cooler and older. 

Stellar Isochrones

A stellar isochrone is the curve on the H-R diagram that represents a population of stars that are about the same age.

 

Information on M80

M80 or Messier 80 is a globular cluster situated in the constellation of Scorpius. M80's spatial diameter is about 95 light-years, but the apparent diameter of M80 is about 10 ft.. The  distance from earth to M80 is about 32,600 light-years. M80 contains several hundred thousand stars, and is one of the more densely populated globular clusters in the Milky Way Galaxy.

 

 Globular Clusters Summarized 

Overall, the collapse of a cloud of Hydrogen, Helium and other, heavier elements, creates a star. Globular Clusters are a collection of densly packed stars that are around the same age in the halo of a galaxy. These stars eventually start loosing heat and start following the main sequence. The main sequence describes the life cycle of a star. The H-R diagram and the CMD compare the temperature or color to the luminosity or magnitude of the star. The curves on these diagrams are called Isochrones.
